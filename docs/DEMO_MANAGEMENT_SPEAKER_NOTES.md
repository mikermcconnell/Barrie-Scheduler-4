# Management Demo Speaker Notes

## Demo Opening
- This platform brings together **planning, analysis, and schedule management** in one place.
- It automatically **ingests daily raw STREETS data**, stores it, and turns it into usable operational information.
- The purpose of this demo is **two-fold**:
  1. to demonstrate **what information is available** for the department
  2. to demonstrate the platform’s **capabilities if there is a pain point to mitigate** — especially a recurring process, repetitive task, or operational annoyance
- The goal is to reduce manual work and give staff **faster operational insight**.
- This will be a high-level overview, but if there is any feature you would like me to focus on afterward, please let me know.
- Today I’ll show:
  - **Transit app data**
  - **School trip planning**
  - **Operations dashboard**
  - **Master schedule editor**

---

## 1. Transit App Data

### Transition
- First, this is the **Transit App Data** workspace.
- This is where we can turn rider movement data into planning insight.

### OD Pairs
- This view shows **origin-destination pairs**.
- It helps us understand **where riders are actually travelling**, not just where they board.
- This is useful for:
  - identifying strong travel markets
  - spotting missed direct connections
  - understanding corridor demand
  - supporting route design decisions

### Insight line
- For management, this helps answer:
  - **Are people travelling where we think they are?**
  - **Are our current route patterns aligned with actual demand?**

### Heatmap
- This is the **heatmap**.
- It shows where rider activity is concentrated across the network.
- Helpful for:
  - identifying demand clusters
  - showing where activity is strongest at a glance
  - supporting service allocation discussions
  - highlighting areas where transit activity may justify closer review

### Insight line
- This gives a quick visual read on:
  - **where transit use is strongest**
  - **where there may be concentration without enough service attention**

### Stop Analysis
- This tab breaks performance down to the **stop level**.
- It helps identify:
  - highest activity stops
  - weaker stops
  - stop-level boarding and alighting patterns
- Helpful for:
  - stop infrastructure prioritization
  - service review
  - customer amenity planning
  - identifying where operational attention is needed

### Insight line
- This helps management see:
  - **which stops matter most**
  - **where improvements could have the biggest customer impact**

### Transfers
- This view highlights **transfer behaviour**.
- It helps us understand where riders are connecting between services.
- Useful for:
  - identifying important transfer hubs
  - seeing where timed connections matter
  - spotting where poor coordination could affect riders
  - supporting network integration decisions

### Insight line
- From a management perspective, this helps answer:
  - **Where do connections matter most?**
  - **Where would schedule coordination have the highest value?**

### Wrap line for Transit App Data
- So this section gives us a much better picture of **how riders use the network**, which supports better planning and investment decisions.

## 2. School Trip

### Transition
- Next is the **school trip workflow**.
- This supports planning for targeted school service in a more structured way.

### Main points
- School trips are operationally important, but they are often hard to manage cleanly.
- This workflow helps planners:
  - build trips
  - review timing
  - keep the service organized
  - make changes more safely

### Management insight
- This is helpful because it:
  - reduces manual setup work
  - improves consistency
  - supports more reliable planning around school demand
  - makes specialized service easier to maintain over time

### Key line
- The value here is not just creating a trip.
- The value is having a **repeatable process** for a service type that is usually complicated and labour-intensive.

### Wrap line
- For management, this means better control over a specialized service area that often creates extra planning effort.

## 3. Operations Dashboard

### Transition
- The next piece is the **Operations Dashboard**.
- This is where recent service performance becomes visible very quickly.

### Overview
- This overview is focused on the **most recent data**.
- It is designed to surface the most useful information first.
- Helpful for:
  - recent performance monitoring
  - identifying service issues
  - spotting trends early
  - supporting evidence-based follow-up

### Insight line
- This helps management quickly answer:
  - **How is service performing right now?**
  - **Where should attention go first?**

### OTP / performance trends
- We can look at schedule reliability and trend information.
- This helps us see:
  - whether service is holding up
  - where issues are concentrated
  - whether recent adjustments are improving performance

### Ridership
- Ridership views help connect operations to customer use.
- This shows not just whether there is a problem, but whether it affects **high-use service**.

### Load profiles / dwell / other tabs
- These tabs provide deeper operational detail.
- Helpful for:
  - understanding crowding
  - passenger activity at stops
  - operator dwell patterns
  - where schedule pressure may be coming from

### Management insight
- The real value is that this helps move from:
  - anecdotal issues
  - to measurable operational patterns

### Wrap line
- For management, this supports:
  - performance review
  - service prioritization
  - stronger conversations about where intervention is needed

## 4. Master Schedule Editor

### Transition
- Finally, this is the **Master Schedule Editor**.
- This is where planners can make detailed schedule changes in a structured way.

### Main points
- This is a block-based schedule view.
- It shows:
  - trips
  - timing
  - recovery
  - cycle structure
  - vehicle and block flow through the day

### Insight line
- This is important because schedule changes are not isolated.
- A small change in one place can affect reliability, recovery, and vehicle use elsewhere.

### What to show
- make a small timing edit
- show how the change is visible
- show the delta / change indication
- show that the planner can understand the impact clearly

### Management insight
- This helps reduce risk in schedule changes by making the effects more visible.
- It supports:
  - more controlled editing
  - better planner productivity
  - clearer understanding of what changed
  - safer refinement of schedules over time

### Key line
- The value here is not just editing times.
- It is giving planners a better tool to manage operational complexity.

### Wrap line
- For management, this supports higher-quality schedules and a more reliable planning workflow.
