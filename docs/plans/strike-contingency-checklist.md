## Operating Model Context

Barrie Transit is operated under contract by **MVT (Miller Vehicle Transit)**. MVT is responsible for day-to-day service delivery including driving, dispatching, vehicle maintenance, and field supervision. The **City of Barrie** is responsible for service planning, scheduling, communications, strategic direction, and Council reporting.

A labour disruption would involve **MVT's unionized employees** (operators, mechanics, dispatchers). Conventional and specialized transit operators are in the **same bargaining unit** — a strike takes down both services entirely. During a strike:

- **MVT management (~11 licensed staff)** would operate skeleton conventional service
- **Specialized transit ceases completely** — taxi vouchers or contracted providers required for dialysis/medical trips
- **City of Barrie** would lead communications, partner coordination, planning, and oversight
- Both parties must coordinate closely — this checklist identifies the responsible party for each task
- MVT has provided their own contingency plan; this City checklist complements it

**Facility ownership:** The City owns the garage and terminal; MVT maintains and operates out of them. Picketing can occur at City-owned facilities.

**Route 8 rationale:** Selected as the skeleton route because it connects the most essential destinations — RVH (hospital), Georgian College, Allandale Waterfront GO station, and downtown Barrie.

**Key open items requiring immediate resolution:**
- [ ] Essential services agreement status — unknown; this is between MVT and their union, not a City mandate. Confirm with MVT whether one exists, as it dictates minimum legal service levels.
- [ ] MVT contract payment terms during a labour disruption — unknown. Review force majeure / labour disruption clauses to determine the City's financial obligations.
- [ ] Public communications strategy for a contractor labour dispute — not yet addressed. Needs to be worked through with Communications and Legal before it's needed.

## Executive Summary

This checklist provides a comprehensive framework for managing a potential labour disruption at Barrie Transit. The contingency plan assumes:

- **Skeleton conventional service:** Route 8 operating on an hourly frequency, operated by MVT management-licensed staff
- **Specialized transit:** Reduced to dialysis and emergency medical trips only
- **All other conventional fixed-route and on-demand services suspended**
- The checklist covers 10 operational areas from pre-strike preparation through post-strike return to full service
